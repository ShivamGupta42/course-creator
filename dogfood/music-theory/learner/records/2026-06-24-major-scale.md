Worked Module 08. Started from the W–W–H–W–W–W–H pattern instead of the letter
names, which landed well for an ear player: I had them play C major up the
keyboard hearing the two half-step spots (E→F and B→C) where there is no black
key between. Then built G major by the same pattern and they hit the moment the
7th note needs to be F♯, not F, to keep the last two steps W then H. They got
why F♮ would sound wrong there.

Stumble: at first they tried to memorize "G major has one sharp" as a fact rather
than derive it from the pattern. Re-derived it once and the fact stuck because it
had a reason. Breakthrough: "the pattern is the scale, the letters just follow."

Open gap: have not built a flat key (F major needs B♭) yet, and have not numbered
the degrees as scale-degree functions. Left F major and degree-numbering as the
next independent task. Did not touch minor.
