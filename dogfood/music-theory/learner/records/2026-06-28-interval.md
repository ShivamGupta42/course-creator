Worked Module 06 (naming intervals) and started Module 14 (building triads). The
semitone-counting drill went fast: they can now say a major 3rd is 4 semitones
and a perfect 5th is 7 without counting frets out loud. The compare-two-fragments
lab (major 3rd C–E vs minor 3rd C–E♭) worked by ear, they heard the minor 3rd as
"sadder" and matched it to the 3-semitone count.

Stumble: when we stacked thirds into a triad, the **diminished** triad (C–E♭–G♭)
threw them. They expected every triad to "sound like a chord" and the diminished
sounded broken/unfinished, so they assumed they'd built it wrong. Showed it is
correct and that the tension is the point (it wants to resolve). Marked triad
shaky in state.

Breakthrough: major triad = major 3rd then minor 3rd (4+3), minor triad = the
reverse (3+4). That arithmetic gave them a non-ear check.

Open gap: diminished/augmented by ear; harmonic-minor raised 7th ♮ from the prior
session is still loose. Next session: re-teach minor's raised 7th at lower load,
then push triad quality recognition faded → independent.
