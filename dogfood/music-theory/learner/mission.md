Plays guitar and a little piano entirely by ear, can copy a riff after a few
listens, but has no idea why the chords of a song belong together or what to call
them. Cannot read a score and does not want to learn staff notation right now.

Wants to be able to sit with a song they like, find its key, work out its chord
progression by ear, and explain *why* those chords fit, for example "that's a
I–V–vi–IV in G, and the V pulls back to the I." Cares most about: intervals (so
two-note moves make sense), the major and minor scales, diatonic chords, and
cadence/function (the "why it resolves" part). Less interested in dense notation
or classical part-writing rules.

Has about six weeks of evenings. Success = can take one named song they already
play, name its key and chords by ear, and justify the progression using function
(tonic / subdominant / dominant), unaided.

The agent should specialize `data-campus`/`data-example` anchors to songs and the
guitar/piano this learner already plays, not to dorm-life defaults, and should
prioritize Track 1 intervals → Track 2 diatonic chords → Track 3 function/cadence
over the full 25 in order.
