A **seventh chord** is a triad with one more third stacked on top, adding the
note a 7th above the root. The 7th's quality names the chord:

- **Major 7th** (Cmaj7 = C E G B): major triad + major 7th, lush and calm.
- **Dominant 7th** (C7 = C E G B♭): major triad + minor 7th (♭7), tense, wants
  to resolve. This is the chord that drives most cadences.
- **Minor 7th** (Cm7 = C E♭ G B♭): minor triad + minor 7th, smooth.
- **Half-diminished** (Cm7♭5 = C E♭ G♭ B♭): diminished triad + minor 7th.

The dominant 7th's ♭7 plus its 3rd form a tritone, the interval whose tension
makes the chord pull toward the tonic.
