A **key signature** is the set of sharps ♯ or flats ♭ that a key always uses, so
they need not be marked on every note. It is a consequence of the major-scale
pattern: each key needs exactly the accidentals that keep the
`W–W–H–W–W–W–H` spacing. C major needs none. G major needs F♯. F major needs B♭.

Sharps are added in the order F C G D A E B; flats in the reverse order
B E A D G C F. A key has either sharps or flats, never both. A note outside the
key (a **borrowed** note, e.g. a ♭VII chord's flat) is written with an explicit
accidental and often a natural ♮ later to cancel it.
