A **triad** is a three-note chord built by stacking two thirds: a root, the note
a 3rd above it, and the note a 5th above the root. The qualities of those thirds
set the chord type:

- **Major** triad: major 3rd then minor 3rd (C E G), 4 + 3 semitones.
- **Minor** triad: minor 3rd then major 3rd (C E♭ G), 3 + 4 semitones.
- **Diminished**: minor 3rd then minor 3rd (C E♭ G♭), 3 + 3, a tense, unstable sound.
- **Augmented**: major 3rd then major 3rd (C E G♯), 4 + 4.

The same three notes in a different vertical order are an **inversion** of the
same triad. Triads are the backbone of chords in popular music.
