**Voice leading** is how the individual notes of one chord move to the notes of
the next. Smooth voice leading keeps common tones held and moves the other notes
by the smallest interval, often a half or whole step. C major to G major share
G, so a singer or top string barely moves while the rest shift a step.

Good voice leading is why a progression sounds connected rather than like blocks
jumping around. On guitar it shows up as choosing a chord shape near the last
one; on piano as letting fingers slide a key instead of leaping. The same three
notes of a triad can be voiced many ways (inversions) to make the move smoother.
