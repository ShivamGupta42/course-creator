The **natural minor scale** uses the step pattern

`W – H – W – W – H – W – W`

which lowers the 3rd, 6th, and 7th degrees relative to the major scale
(♭3, ♭6, ♭7), giving the darker minor sound. Every major scale has a **relative
minor** built on its 6th degree using the exact same notes: C major and A minor
share C D E F G A B.

**Harmonic minor** raises the 7th degree back up (a natural ♮ on that note) so
the chord built on degree 5 becomes major and pulls strongly home. **Melodic
minor** raises the 6th and 7th going up and lowers them coming down.
