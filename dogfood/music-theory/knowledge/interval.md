An **interval** is the distance between two notes. The smallest distance is the
**half step** (semitone): one note to its nearest neighbor, e.g. E to F, or C to
C♯. Two half steps make a **whole step** (whole tone).

Intervals have two parts to their name: a **quality** (major, minor, perfect,
diminished, augmented) and a **number** (2nd, 3rd, 5th …). The number counts
letter names; the quality fixes the exact semitone count. A major 3rd is 4
semitones (C to E); a minor 3rd is 3 (C to E♭). A perfect 5th is 7 semitones
(C to G). Intervals are the raw material of scales and chords.
