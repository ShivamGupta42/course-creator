**Diatonic chords** are the chords you get by building a triad on each note of a
scale using only that scale's notes. In a major key the qualities are fixed:

`I  ii  iii  IV  V  vi  vii°`
(major, minor, minor, major, major, minor, diminished)

In C major: C, Dm, Em, F, G, Am, B°. Roman numerals name the chord by its scale
degree, uppercase for major and lowercase for minor, with ° for diminished. The
pattern is the same in every major key because it comes from the scale's
interval spacing, not the letters. This is why a I–IV–V or I–V–vi–IV works the
same in any key: it is the same numerals transposed.
