The **circle of fifths** arranges the twelve keys around a clock so that each
step clockwise goes up a perfect 5th and adds one sharp ♯; each step
counter-clockwise goes up a perfect 4th and adds one flat ♭. C sits at the top
with no accidentals. Moving clockwise: C, G, D, A, E, B … Moving
counter-clockwise: C, F, B♭, E♭, A♭ …

It is a map: keys next to each other share almost all their notes, so songs move
between neighbors easily. The relative minor sits inside the circle on the same
spoke as its major. The circle also predicts the order accidentals appear in a
key signature.
