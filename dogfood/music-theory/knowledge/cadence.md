A **cadence** is the chord move that ends a musical phrase, like punctuation. The
common types:

- **Authentic** (V → I): the strong full stop. With a dominant 7th (V7 → I) the
  ♭7's tritone makes the pull strongest.
- **Plagal** (IV → I): the "amen" ending, softer.
- **Half** (… → V): ends on tension, a comma, expecting more.
- **Deceptive** (V → vi): sets up the V → I resolution then sidesteps to vi,
  a surprise that keeps the phrase open.

A cadence is where chord function becomes audible: the dominant's tension and
the tonic's release are the whole effect.
