**Rhythm** is the pattern of sound and silence in time; **meter** is the regular
pulse those sounds sit on, grouped into **bars** of a fixed number of **beats**.
A time signature like 4/4 means four beats per bar, each a quarter note; 3/4 is
the waltz feel of three.

Meter decides where chords change (usually on strong beats, often the downbeat of
a bar) and where stress falls. Syncopation places accents off the expected
beats. For an ear player, hearing the meter is how you know when the next chord
arrives, even before you know which chord it is. Rhythm is orthogonal to pitch:
the same progression can carry many rhythms.
