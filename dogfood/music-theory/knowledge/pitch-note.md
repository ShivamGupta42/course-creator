A **pitch** is how high or low a sound is, set by how fast the string or air
vibrates (its frequency). A **note** is a named pitch: one of the twelve names
that repeat every octave. Western music uses seven letter names A B C D E F G,
plus the five in-between pitches written with a sharp ♯ (one half step up) or a
flat ♭ (one half step down). A natural ♮ cancels a previous ♯ or ♭.

Doubling the frequency gives the **same note name one octave higher**: A at 220
Hz and A at 440 Hz are both "A." That is why the twelve names repeat.
