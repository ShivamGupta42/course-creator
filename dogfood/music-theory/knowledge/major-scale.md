The **major scale** is seven notes built from one starting note (the tonic) by a
fixed pattern of whole (W) and half (H) steps:

`W – W – H – W – W – W – H`

Starting on C and applying the pattern lands on only the white keys:
C D E F G A B C, with no ♯ or ♮ needed. Start on G and the pattern forces one
sharp, F♯, to keep the same spacing. The pattern, not the letters, defines the
sound; every major scale is the same intervals transposed. The notes get
**scale-degree numbers** 1–7, which later name chords.
