Net worth is everything you own minus everything you owe, measured at a moment in
time. It is the scoreboard that ties budgeting, saving, debt payoff, and
investing into one number you can track.

Formally, `net worth = Σ(assets) − Σ(liabilities)`. Assets are cash, account
balances, investments, and the resale value of big possessions; liabilities are
loan and card balances. The number can be negative early on, which is normal when
student or card debt outweighs savings. What matters is the direction over time:
a budget with a positive savings rate and a shrinking debt balance pushes net
worth up month after month.
