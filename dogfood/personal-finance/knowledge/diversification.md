Diversification means spreading money across many holdings so no single failure
sinks you. Owning one company exposes you to that company's fate; owning a broad
index fund of thousands of companies means any one going to zero barely moves the
total.

The mechanism is that the prices of different assets do not move in perfect
lockstep, so their ups and downs partly cancel, lowering the swings of the whole
without lowering the expected long-run return as much. A broad index fund is the
simplest way to hold this: one purchase buys a slice of the whole market at very
low cost. Diversification reduces the risk that comes from any single holding; it
does not remove the risk that the whole market falls together.
