An emergency fund is cash set aside in an accessible account to cover essential
expenses when income stops or a large unplanned bill arrives, so a shock does not
force new high-interest debt. It is insurance you self-fund, not an investment.

The common target is three to six months of essential expenses, computed from
your own numbers: `target = monthly essential spending × months of coverage`. If
essentials are $3,000/month, a three-month fund is $9,000. It is held in a liquid,
low-risk place (a high-yield savings account), not in stocks, because the whole
point is that it is there on the day you need it.
