Asset allocation is how you split your portfolio across asset classes, mainly
stocks (higher expected return, larger swings) and bonds (steadier, lower
return). It is the biggest lever on how much your portfolio grows and how much it
lurches in a bad year.

The dominant input is your timeline: money you need in thirty years can ride out
stock swings, money you need in two years cannot. A rough starting heuristic is
to hold a stock percentage near `110 − your age`, with the rest in bonds, then
adjust for how much volatility you can stomach. Allocation drifts as markets move,
so it is reset, or rebalanced, on a schedule back to the target mix.
