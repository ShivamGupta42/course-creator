Interest is the price of borrowing money or the reward for lending it, charged as
a percentage of the balance. APR (annual percentage rate) is that price stated as
a yearly rate, and on most credit cards it includes the lender's required fees so
two cards can be compared on one number.

A card's monthly periodic rate is roughly `APR ÷ 12`. On a $2,000 balance at 24%
APR, one month of interest is about `2000 × (0.24 ÷ 12) = $40`. Because card
interest is charged on the running balance, carrying it means paying interest on
interest, which is why the APR on revolving debt matters far more than the APR on
a fixed loan you pay down on schedule.
