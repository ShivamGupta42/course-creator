Cash flow is the money moving into and out of your hands over a period, usually a
month. Money in is take-home pay plus any other income; money out is every bill,
purchase, and transfer. The plain question it answers: at month end, did more
come in than went out?

Formally, net cash flow over a period equals total inflows minus total outflows:
`net = Σ(income) − Σ(spending)`. A positive number means you kept some; a
negative number means you drew down savings or added debt to cover the gap.
Timing matters as much as the total, because a bill due before payday can cause a
shortfall even in a month that nets positive.
