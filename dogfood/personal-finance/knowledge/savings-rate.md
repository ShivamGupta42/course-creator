Your savings rate is the share of your take-home pay you keep instead of spend,
counting money sent to savings, investments, and extra debt payoff. It is the
single number that most predicts how fast your finances improve, because it sets
both how much you bank and how little you need to live on.

Formally, `savings rate = amount saved ÷ take-home pay`. Saving $800 out of
$4,000 take-home is a 20% rate. A higher rate compounds twice: it grows the pile
faster and shrinks the target the pile has to cover. A rate of 0% or negative
means you are spending everything or borrowing to spend.
