A financial scam is a deception engineered to make you hand over money or account
access, usually by manufacturing urgency and impersonating someone you trust: a
bank, the tax authority, an employer, or a romantic contact. Phishing is the
common delivery, a message that pushes you to click a link or share a code.

There is no formula here; there is a decision rule. The reliable tells are
pressure to act immediately, a request for a password, one-time code, gift card,
or wire transfer, a sender address or link that does not match the real
institution, and a payment method that cannot be reversed. The defensive move is
always the same: stop, do not use the contact details in the message, and reach
the institution through a number or app you already trust. Legitimate
institutions do not ask for your one-time code or demand gift cards.
