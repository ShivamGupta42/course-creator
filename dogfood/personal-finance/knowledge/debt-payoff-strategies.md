When you carry several debts, you pay the minimum on all of them and put every
extra dollar toward one. Which one defines the strategy. The avalanche method
targets the highest APR first, which costs the least interest overall. The
snowball method targets the smallest balance first, which clears whole debts
sooner and gives motivating early wins.

Mathematically the avalanche minimizes total interest paid, because interest
accrues fastest on the highest rate, so `extra → max(APR)` is the cost-optimal
order. The snowball trades a little extra interest for psychological momentum.
Both work because the extra payment, not the order, is what retires debt; the
order only changes the total cost and the timing of the wins.
