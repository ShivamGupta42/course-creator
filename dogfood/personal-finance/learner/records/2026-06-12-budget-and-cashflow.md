First real session. Worked Modules 01-03 plus a peek at net worth. We pulled one
month of the actual bank and card statements instead of guessing. Money in was
$4,200 take-home; money out was about $4,090, so cash flow netted barely positive,
which surprised them since it "feels like nothing is left." The leak was $300/month
in forgotten subscriptions and one card's minimum being treated as the whole
payment.

Built a 50/30/20 budget from their own numbers: ~$2,100 needs, ~$1,260 wants,
~$840 to savings and extra debt payoff. They cut two subscriptions on the spot.

Computed net worth honestly: about $600 cash + small 401(k) of $0 − $14,000 cards
= roughly −$13,400. They took the negative number better than expected once it was
framed as a starting line, not a grade.

Open gaps: the budget is on paper, not yet survived a live month, so budgeting
stays `solid` only provisionally. Savings rate confused them: they did not count
extra debt payoff as "saving," so they think their rate is lower than it is. Left
that as the first thing to fix next session. Did not touch compound interest or
the 401(k) match yet.
