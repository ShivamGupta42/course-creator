Second session, focused on the debt. Worked Module 06 (reading a debt) and started
Module 10 (avalanche vs snowball). Used their real cards: Card A ~$8,000 at 24%
APR, Card B ~$6,000 at 19% APR.

Breakthrough: when we computed the monthly interest, Card A alone is about
`8000 × 0.24 ÷ 12 = $160/month` and Card B about `6000 × 0.19 ÷ 12 ≈ $95/month`,
so roughly $255/month is going to interest before any progress. Seeing that number
made the "why minimums trap you" point land harder than any explanation. APR moved
to `solid`.

Stumbled on strategy. They want snowball (clear the smaller Card B first for the
early win), but had not seen the avalanche cost. We did not finish the comparison,
so debt-payoff stays `shaky`. Next session: show the actual dollar and time
difference between avalanche (Card A first, the 24%) and snowball (Card B first),
using their $840/month payoff budget, then let them choose with eyes open.
