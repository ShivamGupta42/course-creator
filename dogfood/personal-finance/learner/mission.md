A 34-year-old with a steady job, take-home pay around $4,200/month, carrying about
$14,000 in credit-card debt spread across two cards (one near 24% APR, one near
19%) and roughly $600 in a checking account as the only cushion. The employer
offers a 401(k) with a 50% match up to 6% of salary, which is currently not being
used at all.

Wants two concrete outcomes within 18 months: clear the $14k of card debt, and
start retirement saving, at minimum capturing the full employer match. Cares most
about a budget that survives a real month, a debt-payoff order that is both
cheap and motivating, and not getting wiped out by a surprise expense along the
way. Allergic to jargon and to anything that needs a spreadsheet they will not
maintain.

Success looks like: a written monthly budget with a savings/payoff rate of at
least 20%, both cards at zero, a starter emergency fund of about one month of
essentials, and the 401(k) contributing enough to get the whole match. Anchors
should specialize to this person's real numbers (the $14k balances, the $4,200
take-home, the 50% match), not to generic dorm-life examples.
