Starting an engineering degree in September and the calculus pre-reqs scare me. I
was fine in school until functions, then I started memorizing steps without seeing
why they worked, and limits never clicked. I want to walk into the first calculus
lecture able to read a derivative as a rate of change and not freeze when a proof
shows up on the board. I have about ten weeks before term.

Success looks like: I can explain to a friend what `f'(a)` measures, compute the
slope of a simple curve at a point by hand, and follow a short induction proof
without getting lost. Probability is a bonus, since my stats course needs it later.

I learn best from a concrete case first. Generic worked examples bounce off me; I
need to see real numbers move before the symbols mean anything. Anchor the examples
in things I actually deal with: splitting rent with flatmates, gym progression, bus
timetables, lab readings.
