Worked Module 05. Opened by reading state: variables and expressions were already
solid, so we went straight to the balancing move rather than re-teaching
substitution. The learner solved `3x + 4 = 19` cleanly: subtract 4, divide by 3,
got `x = 5`, and checked it by substituting back. Good instinct to verify.

Stumble: `−2x + 7 = 1`. They subtracted 7 fine to get `−2x = −6`, then divided and
wrote `x = −3` instead of `3`, dropping the fact that a negative over a negative is
positive. We re-derived it on the number line: `−2x = −6` means "negative two of
something is six below zero", so the something is positive three. Once they saw the
two negatives cancel, the next two problems were clean.

Open gap: still effortful when the coefficient is negative, and they have not yet
met an equation with the unknown on both sides. Leave "unknown on both sides" as the
next independent task and re-test the negative-coefficient case at the start of next
session before introducing functions properly. Marked linear-equations `shaky`, not
`solid`, because the sign slip recurs under any time pressure.
